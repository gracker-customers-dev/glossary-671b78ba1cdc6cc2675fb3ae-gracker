biometric authentication

A security process that relies on unique biological characteristics of an individual to verify identity.

Description

Biometric authentication is a method of verifying an individual's identity based on their unique physical or behavioral traits. In the context of network security protocols, it involves using biometric data such as fingerprints, facial recognition, iris scans, or voice patterns to authenticate users before granting them access to secure systems or information. This approach offers a higher level of security compared to traditional password-based systems, as biometric traits are much harder to replicate or steal. Biometric authentication typically involves capturing the biometric data, creating a mathematical representation or template, and comparing this template against the stored data during the authentication process. In addition to enhancing security, biometric systems can also improve user convenience by eliminating the need for passwords, which can be forgotten or compromised. However, the use of biometric data raises privacy concerns and requires robust security measures to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access or breaches.

Examples

  • Fingerprint scanners used in smartphones for secure unlocking.
  • Facial recognition technology employed in airport security systems.

Additional Information

  • Biometric data is often stored in encrypted formats to enhance security.
  • Regulations such as GDPR may impact how biometric data is collected and used.
