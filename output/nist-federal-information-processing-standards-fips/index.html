NIST Federal Information Processing Standards (FIPS)

FIPS are public standards developed by NIST for use in federal computer systems.

Description

The NIST Federal Information Processing Standards (FIPS) are a set of standards developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) for federal agencies to use in their information systems. These standards are aimed at ensuring the security and interoperability of information systems within the federal government. FIPS encompasses a variety of areas including cryptographic algorithms, data formats, and security protocols. In the context of network security protocols, FIPS specifies requirements for encryption and key management that federal agencies must adhere to, particularly when handling sensitive information. For example, FIPS 140-2 outlines the security requirements for cryptographic modules, while FIPS 197 provides the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) specification. Compliance with FIPS is often required for federal contracts and is considered a benchmark for security in many sectors, influencing private sector practices as well. Organizations seeking to do business with the federal government often must implement FIPS-compliant solutions to ensure that they meet the necessary security standards.

Examples

  • FIPS 140-2: Security requirements for cryptographic modules.
  • FIPS 197: Specification of the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES).

Additional Information

  • FIPS standards apply to federal agencies and by extension to contractors working with them.
  • Non-compliance with FIPS can limit access to federal contracts and use of federal systems.
