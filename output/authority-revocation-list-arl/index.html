authority revocation list (ARL)

A list that identifies digital certificates that have been revoked by a certificate authority.

Description

An Authority Revocation List (ARL) is a critical component in the framework of network security protocols, particularly those relying on digital certificates for authentication and secure communication, such as SSL/TLS. The ARL is issued by a Certificate Authority (CA) and contains the serial numbers of certificates that have been revoked before their scheduled expiration date. This revocation can occur for various reasons, such as if the private key of a certificate has been compromised, if the certificate was issued based on fraudulent information, or if the entity no longer requires the certificate. When a client or server receives a certificate, it must check the ARL to ensure that the certificate is still valid and has not been revoked. This process helps maintain the integrity of secure communications and prevents the misuse of revoked certificates. To keep ARLs updated, they are often published at regular intervals, and network devices must be capable of accessing these lists to verify certificate validity.

Examples

  • An ARL for an organization that lists revoked certificates after a security breach.
  • A regularly updated ARL published by a CA that clients reference during SSL/TLS handshake.

Additional Information

  • ARLs are often used in conjunction with Online Certificate Status Protocol (OCSP) for real-time revocation checking.
  • The management of ARLs is crucial for maintaining trust in public key infrastructures (PKIs).
