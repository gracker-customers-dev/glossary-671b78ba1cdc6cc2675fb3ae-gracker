RFC

Request for Comments

Description

RFC, or Request for Comments, is a type of publication from the leading technical development and standards-setting bodies for the internet, specifically the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) and the Internet Society (ISOC). RFCs are a series of memoranda describing methods, behaviors, investigations, or advances related to the operation of the internet and internet-connected systems. They cover a wide array of topics, including network protocols, procedures, programs, and concepts, and they serve as a primary source of information for developers and engineers working in the field of network security and other areas. Each RFC is assigned a unique number and can be proposed as a standard. Some RFCs become Internet Standards, while others are informational or experimental. The process of creating an RFC ensures that contributions from the community are documented, shared, and openly discussed, fostering collaboration and innovation in internet technologies.

Examples

  • RFC 791 - Internet Protocol (IP)
  • RFC 5246 - The Transport Layer Security (TLS) Protocol Version 1.2

Additional Information

  • RFCs are maintained and published by the RFC Editor.
  • The term 'Request for Comments' reflects the collaborative nature of the document creation process.
