*-property

A property in network security protocols that prevents unauthorized access or information leakage.

Description

The *-property, often referred to as the star property, is a fundamental concept in the realm of network security protocols and access control models, particularly in the context of mandatory access control (MAC) systems. It stipulates that a subject (e.g., a user or process) at a higher security level cannot read data from a lower security level, thereby preventing unauthorized access to sensitive information. This property is essential in maintaining the confidentiality of data by ensuring that users cannot access information they are not authorized to see. In practice, the *-property is applied in environments where data classification is critical, such as government or military systems. By enforcing this property, organizations can reduce the risk of data breaches and information leaks, ensuring that sensitive data remains protected from unauthorized disclosure. Furthermore, the *-property is often paired with the simple security property (no read up), which complements its functionality by restricting the reading and writing capabilities of users based on their clearance levels.

Examples

  • A military personnel with top-secret clearance cannot access confidential documents categorized at a lower clearance level.
  • In a multi-level secure database, a user with access to high-security data is prohibited from reading low-security records.

Additional Information

  • The *-property is a key aspect of the Bell-LaPadula security model.
  • It helps in enforcing a clear separation between data of varying sensitivity levels in secure systems.
