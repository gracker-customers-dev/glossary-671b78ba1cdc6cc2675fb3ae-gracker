association

A logical connection established between two or more entities in a network for the purpose of communication and data exchange.

Description

In the context of network security protocols, an 'association' refers to the establishment of a logical connection or relationship between two entities, such as a client and a server, or between two devices within a network. This connection is crucial for facilitating secure communication, allowing for the exchange of data while maintaining integrity, confidentiality, and authenticity. Associations can be temporary or long-lived, depending on the protocol and the nature of the communication. For example, in the context of wireless networks, an association occurs when a client device connects to a wireless access point, establishing parameters for data exchange. Security protocols often leverage associations to manage keys, sessions, and user credentials, ensuring that the communication remains secure against unauthorized access. The maintenance and management of these associations are vital for the overall security and efficiency of network operations.

Examples

  • An association between a smartphone and a Wi-Fi access point, allowing for data transmission.
  • An association established during a TLS handshake between a client and a server to initiate a secure session.

Additional Information

  • Associations may include parameters such as security keys, session identifiers, and timeouts.
  • The process of establishing an association often involves authentication methods to verify the identity of the entities involved.
