brute force

A method of gaining unauthorized access by systematically trying all possible combinations of passwords or encryption keys.

Description

Brute force attacks are a straightforward yet effective method used by attackers to gain unauthorized access to systems and networks. In a brute force attack, the attacker attempts to guess a password or encryption key by systematically trying every possible combination until the correct one is found. This method can be applied to various types of security protocols, including user account logins, Wi-Fi passwords, and encrypted files. The effectiveness of a brute force attack largely depends on the complexity and length of the password or key being targeted. Short and simplistic passwords can be compromised relatively quickly, while longer and more complex passwords can significantly increase the time required to successfully execute a brute force attack. To defend against such attacks, security measures like account lockout policies, rate limiting, and the use of multi-factor authentication (MFA) are recommended to mitigate the risk and enhance security.

Examples

  • An attacker uses a script to attempt all possible combinations of a 4-digit PIN to access a smartphone.
  • A hacker employs a brute force tool to crack a user's password on a web application by trying millions of combinations.

Additional Information

  • Brute force attacks can be categorized as either online or offline, depending on whether the attacker has direct access to the system or is working from a stolen hash.
  • Modern security practices recommend using complex passwords and implementing account lockout mechanisms to thwart brute force attempts.
